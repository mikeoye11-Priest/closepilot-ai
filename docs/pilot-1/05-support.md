## Priority and response targets

| Priority | Example | Initial response target | Action |
|---|---|---:|---|
| P0 Security/data | Wrong-client access, suspected breach, data loss | 30 minutes in supported hours | Stop processing, preserve evidence, notify both incident contacts |
| P1 Pilot blocked | Cannot sign in, upload or export; material totals wrong | 2 supported hours | Workaround or recovery plan; pause affected workflow |
| P2 Material defect | Incorrect finding/evidence or broken review action | 1 supported day | Log, assess pilot impact and schedule fix |
| P3 Question/improvement | Guidance, wording or future enhancement | 2 supported days | Answer or add to pilot backlog |

Targets are pilot service objectives, not contractual service levels unless included in the signed Pilot Agreement.

## What to include in a support request

- firm and client code, without unnecessary personal data;
- time and timezone;
- page or workflow step;
- expected and actual result;
- file type and original accounting system;
- screenshot with personal data redacted where possible; and
- whether the review can continue safely.

Do not email client finance exports unless the incident contact explicitly provides an approved secure transfer route.

## Incident first response

1. Stop uploads and review activity if continued use may increase harm.
2. Do not delete logs, screenshots or affected records.
3. Contact both incident contacts using the route above.
4. Record who noticed the issue, when, affected workspace and known data.
5. ClosePilot will acknowledge, contain, investigate and provide updates.
6. The pilot firm, as Controller, decides whether regulatory or data-subject notification is required.
