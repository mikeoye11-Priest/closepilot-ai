## 3. Documented instructions

The Processor will process personal data only:

- to provide and support the pilot service described above;
- on the Controller's documented instructions; or
- where required by applicable law, after informing the Controller unless the law prohibits notice.

The Processor will immediately inform the Controller if, in its reasonable opinion, an instruction infringes applicable data-protection law.

## 4. Confidentiality

The Processor will ensure that people authorised to process personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations, receive access only where necessary and are informed of the pilot's restricted scope.

## 5. Security

The Processor will maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures proportionate to the processing risk. Before signature, the parties must complete Schedule 2 with measures actually operating in staging. No security certification or control should be claimed unless it has been verified.

## 6. Sub-processors

The Controller gives [specific/general] written authorisation for the sub-processors in Schedule 3. The Processor will:

- give at least [30] days' notice of a proposed change;
- allow the Controller to object on reasonable data-protection grounds;
- impose equivalent data-protection obligations on each sub-processor; and
- remain responsible for the sub-processor's performance of those obligations.

No restricted international transfer may occur unless the parties have documented a lawful transfer mechanism and completed any required risk assessment.

## 7. Assistance

Taking account of the nature of processing and information available, the Processor will reasonably assist the Controller with:

- data-subject requests;
- security and breach obligations;
- data-protection impact assessments and prior consultation; and
- demonstrating compliance with applicable data-protection obligations.

## 8. Personal-data breaches

The Processor will notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal-data breach affecting pilot data. The notice will provide available information about the nature of the breach, affected data and people, likely consequences, mitigation and a contact point. Information may be supplied in phases. The Controller remains responsible for deciding whether notification to the ICO or affected people is required.

## 9. Return and deletion

At the Controller's choice, on expiry or termination the Processor will return or securely delete personal data and existing copies unless applicable law requires retention. The agreed operational deletion window is [NUMBER] days. Backup expiry is [NUMBER] days. The Processor will confirm completion in writing on request.

## 10. Audit and information

The Processor will provide information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with these terms and permit reasonable audits or inspections. Unless an urgent security concern requires otherwise, the Controller will give reasonable notice and avoid unnecessary disruption or exposure of other customers' confidential information.

## 11. Controller responsibilities

The Controller will:

- have a lawful basis and all necessary authority to provide the pilot data;
- give transparent information to affected people where required;
- minimise uploads to data necessary for the agreed review;
- maintain accurate user and access instructions; and
- assess ClosePilot outputs using qualified professional judgement before relying on them.

## 12. Pilot limitations

ClosePilot supports review and does not replace professional accounting, audit, tax or legal judgement. The Controller remains responsible for client decisions, statutory submissions and sign-off. Commercial liability, warranty, confidentiality and termination provisions must be set out in the main Pilot Agreement.
